Why reading books is neceassry?

  • Knowledge and Learning: Books are a valuable source of knowledge and information. They cover a wide range of topics, from history and science to art and literature. By reading books, you can explore different subjects, expand your understanding of the world and continuously learn new things.
  • Language and Communication Skills: Reading books helps improve language skills, vocabulary, grammar, and comprehension. It exposes you to different writing styles, sentence structures and literary techniques, enhancing your own writing and communication skills.
  • Imagination and Creativity: Books make you imagine things, take you to new places and come up with new ideas. They help you see picture in your mind about the characters and the places and make you come up with new ideas of your own.
  • Leisure and Entertainment: Reading books is a form of entertainment at any point of time. It allows you to immerse yourself in captivating narratives, stimulating excitement, and enjoyment.

Here are few recommended books that everyone should read

The Courage to be disliked

The Courage to be disliked is a personal development book by Ichiro Kishimi that shares a story of an unhappy man who goes to a philospher who guides him on how happiness is in your own hands. The book is based on the work of 19th century physcologist Akfred Adler, who established that happiness lies in the hands of each human individual and does not depend on past trauams. The book is written as a Socratic conversation between the philospher and the young man.

The Physcology of money

The Physcology of money is a book by Morgan Housel that explores how human behavior influences our financial decisions. The book uses stories to illustrate phsycological principles and timeless lessons on wealth, greed, and happiness. The book reveals the thought processes that happen before financial investing and the most significant factors contributing to a person's financial success. The book argues that understanding human behavior is more important than understanding the mathematical details of money.

The Atomic Habits

Atomic Habits by James Clear is a comprehensive, practical guide on how to change your habits and get 1% better every day. Using a framework called the Four Laws of Behavior Change, Atomic Habits teaches readers a simple set of rules for creating good habits and breaking bad ones. It is a guide on how to change your habits and get 1% better every day by mastering small, incrmental changes in your daily routines. This book also explains how your habits are a reflection of your identity and how to align them with your goals and values
